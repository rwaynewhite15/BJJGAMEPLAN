BJJ Gameplan

Top Positions

Guard

Gameplan:

  • Maintain posture and control your opponent's movements.
  • Keep arms from crossing center of opponent's body and keep shoulders below opponent's hips.
  • Grip near opponent's hips, dig in elbows to pry closed guard apart.
  • Knee Slice Pass
  • Leg Lace Pass

Side Control

Gameplan:

  • Heavy pressure in Kesa Gatame
  • Look for Head and Arm Choke, Lappel Ezekiel Choke
  • Shift hips to Reverse Kesa Gatame
  • Knee on Belly
  • Transition to mount or back control if the opportunity arises.

Mount

Gameplan:

  • Maintain a high mount to limit your opponent's escape options.
  • Use your weight to control your opponent's upper body.
  • Look for submissions such as armbars, chokes, and mounted triangles.
  • Transition to back control if your opponent tries to escape.
Bottom Positions

Guard

Gameplan:

  • Break your opponent's posture and limit their movement.
  • Look for sweeps, submissions, or transitions.
  • Use guard retention techniques to prevent your opponent from passing.
  • Stay active and look for opportunities to attack.

Side Control

Gameplan:

  • Keep your frames strong to prevent your opponent from applying pressure.
  • Look for opportunities to recover guard or create space for escapes.
  • Use hip escapes and bridging techniques to create movement.
  • Stay aware of your opponent's attempts to submit you.

Rear Mount

Gameplan:

  • Protect your neck and avoid giving up easy chokes.
  • Hand fight to control your opponent's grips.
  • Look for opportunities to escape by turning into your opponent.
  • Stay calm and use proper technique to prevent being submitted.

Stand-Up Techniques

Takedowns

  • Double leg takedown: Shoot in, secure both legs, and drive your opponent to the ground.
  • Single leg takedown: Isolate one leg, lift, and drive your opponent down.
  • Hip toss: Use your hip as leverage to throw your opponent over.
  • Inside trip: Hook your opponent's leg with yours and drive them down.

Grip Fighting

  • Establish strong grips on your opponent's gi or arms.
  • Break your opponent's grips to gain control.
  • Use grip fighting to set up takedowns or guard passes.
  • Maintain dominant grips to control the pace of the fight.
